Our Research

Rwanda: Unemployment and the Underrated Power of Agriculture

Rwanda, a rapidly developing nation in East Africa, faces significant unemployment challenges. As of 2024, the national unemployment rate stands at approximately 16.5% (per World Bank data), with youth unemployment reaching up to 20%. This is exacerbated by a growing population and limited industrial opportunities, leaving many skilled individuals without stable work.

Despite this, agriculture remains a cornerstone of Rwanda's economy, employing over 70% of the workforce and contributing about 30% to GDP. However, it's often underrated as a sector for innovation and job creation. Traditional farming methods are labor-intensive but low-yield, leading to underemployment. INSIGHT LAB's Agriconnect project addresses this by modernizing agriculture—integrating tech like drones for pest control and apps for market access—to create high-value jobs in agribusiness, processing, and logistics.

  • Key Insights: Rwanda's government recognizes agriculture's potential, with initiatives like the National Agriculture Policy aiming to boost productivity. Yet, without tech upgrades, the sector struggles to attract youth, who see it as outdated.
  • Agriconnect's Role: By piloting in Rwanda, we could reduce unemployment by 5-10% in target areas through skill training and tech adoption, proving agriculture's untapped potential.

Our Project: Agriconnect

Agriconnect is our flagship initiative aimed at revolutionizing job creation in agriculture. By modernizing traditional farming practices with cutting-edge technology—like AI-driven crop monitoring, automated irrigation systems, and blockchain for supply chain transparency—we're creating sustainable jobs in rural and urban areas alike. Imagine a world where farmers connect directly with consumers, reducing waste and boosting economies!

  • Key Features: Real-time data analytics, mobile apps for farmers, and community training programs.
  • Impact: Generating thousands of jobs by making agriculture efficient, accessible, and profitable.

In challenge 2 which is Discover Africa, we had to choose an African community and tell a story the world has not heard about that community so our think tank chose The Herero community from Namibia. We found that the Herero community of Namibia, known for their vibrant traditional dress, has a history the world rarely hears. They endured one of the first genocides of the 20th century under German colonial rule but have shown remarkable resilience. Today, they preserve their language, traditions, and pastoral lifestyle, with Herero women playing a key role in passing down culture. Their story is one of strength, survival, and cultural pride.
